In writing this, I'm relinquishing myself of my thoughts and feelings on the civil rights movement against violence, and systemic racism against black people. As a forenote, it's relevant to state that I'm in full agreement with the movement's philosophy, in its entirety do I believe that black people should not have to clash with racial contention solely resulting from the bias of an authoritative figure, regardless of whether there is a statistical, or empirical imperative to lead to such behaviour.

Thus, in light of the recent protests, and under the cloudiness of proceeding riots, the question is begged in contrasting the largely insensitive, and exteriorly hypocritical behaviour that ensued (and is ensuing) in the riots. It becomes immediately obvious to any onlooker that when there are (amongst) two coaligned forces--spawned from the same antecedent, and lead mostly by alleged shareholders in fair civil rights for black people--and yet there are conflicting consequences of each force's application, something must've gone wrong?

Something must've gone wrong. And in analysing this juxtaposition, I propose two onlooks: the simplistic and superficial, and the view that there is much more grounded in the basis of the riots--influenced, inadvertently, for pushing an agenda that is perhaps not formally understood by those participating.
